Ellos nos recomiendan

Our taxi driver recommended Pancho’s restaurant and Pinup’s Bar and Grill was next door and looked cleaner with a better menu. The tacos were not the typical street tacos you might expect, they were oversized soft tacos stuffed with lettuce and vegetables deliciously fried fish and chicken and more food than we could eat. Maybe it wasn’t as “authentic” as some local fare but for about $25 we 4 Americans loved this fresh, clean food — it was a nice surprise - and yes like most tourist based establishments in Mazatlán everyone spoke English (even though I speak Spanish).
